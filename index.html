Our Legacy

The Legacy
of Kashi

✦

For over five millennia, the sacred city of Varanasi — Kashi — has been the cradle of India's finest textile artistry. Banarasi Sanskriti carries forward this magnificent legacy, bringing you handwoven sarees that embody the soul of an ancient civilization.

Each saree is a masterpiece born from the hands of our artisan families, who have perfected the art of weaving intricate gold and silver zari into the purest silks. Every thread tells a story of devotion, heritage, and timeless beauty.

Our Journey

About Banarasi Sanskriti

✦

Welcome to Banarasi Sanskriti, where heritage meets contemporary elegance. Rooted in the spiritual and cultural heart of Varanasi, we are dedicated to preserving, celebrating, and delivering the timeless art of traditional Indian weaves.

Our journey is a tapestry woven from generations of expertise, an unyielding passion for authentic fabrics, and a deep-seated respect for the artisans who bring these textile masterpieces to life. From the intricate folds of a classic Banarasi saree to the refined grace of modern cultural attire, Banarasi Sanskriti bridges the gap between historical legacy and global fashion.

Behind Every Thread

The Art of Handloom

✦
01

Design & Pattern Making

Master artisans hand-sketch intricate motifs inspired by Mughal jalis, sacred lotus blooms, and Kashi's temple architecture.

02

Silk & Zari Selection

Only the finest mulberry silk and authentic gold & silver zari threads are chosen — tested for purity and lustre.

03

Dyeing & Warping

Natural and heritage dyes infuse the silk with deep, lasting colors before the warp threads are carefully prepared.

04

Handloom Weaving

Each saree takes 15 to 30 days of dedicated handloom weaving — every knot, every thread placed with precision and love.

05

Finishing & Quality

Rigorous quality inspection ensures every saree meets the exacting standards of Banarasi Sanskriti before reaching you.

Know Your Saree

Saree Specifications

✦

Fabric Type

High-grade mulberry silk is the standard. Quality is measured by the "ply" (twist) of the thread, which determines the fabric's weight, texture, and longevity.

Zari Types

Real Zari Silver & gold threads — the highest investment & most precious
Tested Zari Copper wire with electroplating — excellent shine, standard choice

Signature Patterns — Butis & Jals

Jhallar Intricate, interlocking floral borders
Bel Creepers and vine-like scrolls
Shikargah Hunting scenes — lions, deer, birds — the pinnacle of weaving skill
Mughal Motifs Persian-influenced florals like the Asharfi (coin) & Paan (leaf)

The Minds Behind the Loom

Our Leadership Team

✦

Mrs. Bandana Chaubey

Director

With over three decades of mastering the intricate dynamics of managing a large, bustling 20-member household, Mrs. Bandana Chaubey brings unparalleled foundational leadership, patience, and operational wisdom to the brand. Her lifelong exposure to cultural traditions has gifted her an intuitive and profound understanding of premium fabrics. Fluent and deeply expressive in both Hindi and Bengali, she grounds the brand in authentic cultural values and ensures that our storytelling remains deeply connected to our roots.

Mr. Shubham Chaubey

Managing Director

Born and raised in the sacred lanes of Varanasi, Shubham’s connection to the loom is personal and lifelong. He possesses a comprehensive, ground-level command over fabric quality, weaver ecosystems, and regional vendor networks. Shubham pairs this deep-rooted traditional knowledge with a highly sophisticated global perspective. A Hospitality graduate, he holds a dual Master’s degree—his first in Marketing Management from India and his second in International Business from the United Arab Emirates—enabling him to seamlessly scale this rich Indian heritage onto the international stage.

Mrs. Bhavana Chaubey

Director of Product & Heritage Sourcing

Belonging to a prominent legacy family in the Banarasi saree industry, Bhavana has textiles running through her veins. Her parental business stands as a premier foundational supplier of high-end sarees to major showrooms and elite corporate merchants across various states of India, with a highly specialized stronghold in the South Indian market. Holding a Master’s degree in Finance, she brilliantly marries fiscal precision with an unmatched eye for design, quality, and wholesale sourcing strategy.

Mr. Anurag

Operations Coordinator

The operational engine of our day-to-day workflow, Anurag manages the logistical and production lifelines of the brand. From coordinating directly with the weavers to supervising final quality checks and managing distribution, he ensures that the artistry of Varanasi reaches your doorstep with absolute perfection and care.
